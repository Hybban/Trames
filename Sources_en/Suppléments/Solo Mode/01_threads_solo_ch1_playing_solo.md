# THREADS — The Solitary Thread
## Solo Supplement

# I. PLAYING SOLO

This supplement is intended for a player who already knows THREADS. It does not re-explain the rules — it adapts and extends them for solo play. Keep the SRD within reach.

---

## What solo changes

In a standard game, the Game Master weaves the world around the heroes. She decides what NPCs know, what hides behind a door, how a threat reacts to your choices. She is the Moirai — invisible, but present.

In solo, you are both at once. You embody your hero *and* you weave the world they traverse. This is not a flaw of the format — it is its very nature. Solo play is not an impoverished standard game. It is another way of telling a story, closer to a logbook than a stage play.

Two practical consequences stem from this duality.

**First consequence — you cannot surprise yourself.** You know what hides behind the door before you even open it. The Narrative Engine (chapter II) is there to work around this problem: it introduces unpredictability where your imagination alone would go in circles.

**Second consequence — nobody validates your decisions.** In a group, the table naturally regulates excesses — a player who forces fate too much in their favor will be reined in by the others. In solo, this regulation comes from you. Respecting your Threads, and especially Atropos's Thread, is the backbone of your narrative honesty. A hero who never gives in to their Sin is not truly playing THREADS.

---

## What this supplement provides

The SRD gives you the base rules for playing solo. This supplement gives you the tools to play *for a long time* and *well*.

- **The Narrative Engine** (chapter II) replaces the Game Master on a daily basis — to decide open questions, generate NPCs, and restart a stalling scene.
- **Campaign Structures** (chapter III) allow you to sustain a story over multiple sessions, with long clocks that give depth and direction to your narrative.
- **The Burnt Thread in Solo** (chapter IV) adapts the ultimate sacrifice rule to the context where nobody else validates the moment.
- **Management Tools** (chapter V) help you keep track between sessions — NPCs, clocks, notes.
- **The Tables** (chapter VI) are your reserve of inspiration. Consult them when the world falls silent.

## One rule before you begin

Before rolling the first die, define one thing: **the promise of your campaign.**

In one or two sentences, write what you want to explore. Not a summary of the plot — the intention. What you want to *feel* or *discover* while playing.

> *Examples:*
> *"I want to explore what it feels like to betray someone you love in order to survive."*
> *"I want to tell the story of a hero who rebuilds their identity after losing everything."*
> *"I want a swashbuckling adventure where every victory comes at a price."*

This promise is not binding — it can evolve. But it will serve as your compass when the Narrative Engine takes you in unexpected directions. If a generated scene does not serve the promise, you have the right to redirect it. The Moirai also have intentions.

Note your promise at the top of your campaign journal, just below your hero's name.
