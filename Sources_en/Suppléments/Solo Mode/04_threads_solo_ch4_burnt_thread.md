# IV. THE BURNT THREAD IN SOLO

The Burnt Thread is defined in the Core Rulebook. This chapter does not re-explain the mechanic — it addresses a question specific to solo play: **when to activate it?**

In a group, the table naturally regulates this moment. A player who would activate the Burnt Thread at the slightest difficulty will be reined in by the others. In solo, this regulation comes from you alone — and that is where the risk lies.

---

## The risk specific to solo

There are two ways to misuse the Burnt Thread in solo, and they are opposites.

**Activating it too early** — out of fear of losing your hero, out of attachment to their traits, out of refusal of a dramatic outcome. In this case, the Burnt Thread becomes insurance, not a sacrifice. It loses all its meaning.

**Never activating it** — out of a desire to preserve every trait, out of a feeling that no situation is *truly* fatal. In this case, the rule does not exist. And with it disappears one of the strongest tensions of THREADS.

The Oracle is there to decide.

---

## Consulting the Oracle

Before activating the Burnt Thread, consult the Oracle of Fate with this precise question:

*"Do the Moirai decide that my story is not over?"*

Roll 1d12 with the **Uncertain** threshold (default).

| Result | Answer | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 9 – 12 | **YES** | The Moirai accept the sacrifice. Activate the Burnt Thread. |
| 5 – 8 | **YES, BUT…** | They accept, but the price is higher. Burn two traits instead of one, or suffer a major narrative consequence in addition. |
| 1 – 4 | **NO** | The Moirai have decided. Your hero does not benefit from the Burnt Thread in this situation. Find another way out — or accept the end. |

### When the result is NO

A NO from the Oracle in the face of a fatal outcome is one of the most powerful moments solo play can produce. It does not necessarily mean the death of the character — it means the Burnt Thread is not the answer here.

Then ask yourself these questions in order:

1. **Is there a resource to sacrifice?** A resource can absorb a consequence instead of a trait (see Core Rulebook).
2. **Is there a Thread to invoke?** Is Lachesis's Thread still available this session? Is the Conviction strong enough to justify spending a token?
3. **Can the failure be narratively integrated?** Some defeats do not kill — they transform. A hero captured, humiliated, or broken remains a hero whose story continues.
4. **Is this the end?** If none of these options is available or satisfying, this campaign ends here. It is a valid outcome. The Moirai have cut the thread.

---

## Choosing the trait to burn

When the Burnt Thread is activated, you must choose which trait to sacrifice. In solo, nobody is there to weigh this choice with you — it is both a freedom and a responsibility.

A few principles to guide this choice:

**Burn what costs.** The Burnt Thread must hurt narratively. Choosing an empty or rarely used trait drains the rule of its meaning. The Moirai do not accept symbolic sacrifices.

**Burn what is coherent.** The sacrificed trait should have a link to the situation — an ability that failed, a conviction that shattered under pressure, a talent that showed its limits at the worst moment.

**Burn what leaves a trace.** After the scene, the fiction must reflect what has been lost. A hero who burned their "Master Archer" trait may never shoot as well again. A hero who burned their "Trust in Humanity" carries it in every future interaction.

### The special case of the Archetype

Burning the Archetype is possible — it is even sometimes the only option that makes sense. But it is the most radical form of sacrifice. The hero survives, but their essence is destroyed. They must rebuild themselves around a new Archetype at d6.

In solo, this moment deserves a pause. Before continuing, write in your campaign journal what this hero *was* before, and what they are beginning to become. This is not optional — it is the condition for the reconstruction to be meaningful.

---

## The Burnt Thread and campaign endings

The Burnt Thread interacts directly with the three campaign endings defined in chapter III.

If the Burnt Thread is activated **before the last segment of the Narrative Arc**, the campaign continues — but the hero emerges from it diminished and transformed. The burned trait is lost for the remainder of the campaign. Its disappearance should be felt in every scene that follows.

If the Burnt Thread is activated **at the last segment**, it is the campaign ending called "The Final Burnt Thread" (chapter III, section 4). The sacrifice closes the story. What has been burned does not rebuild — not in this campaign.

---

## A rule of honesty

The Burnt Thread in solo relies entirely on your good faith. The Oracle helps you decide, but it cannot prevent you from re-rolling the die until you get a YES, or from ignoring a NO that bothers you.

Remember the promise of your campaign (chapter I). If the Burnt Thread betrays this promise — if it erases a consequence that should have existed — then the Moirai have not been respected. And a story where the Moirai are not respected is not a thread. It is a draft.
