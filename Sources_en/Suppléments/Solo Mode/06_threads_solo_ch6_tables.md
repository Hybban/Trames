# VI. TABLES

The tables are reservoirs of inspiration, not oracles. Consult them when the world falls silent, when a scene stalls, or when you need an element that your imagination alone cannot produce. Ignore results that do not serve your story — re-roll, combine, or interpret freely.

All tables use the d12, consistent with the rest of the system.

---

## TABLE A — ACTION & OBJECT
*From the Core Rulebook*

To give direction to a scene or an intention to an event. Roll once for the Verb, once for the Noun — or only once if you need just one column.

| d12 | Verb (Action) | Noun (Subject) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Destroy / Break | Secret / Truth |
| 2 | Protect / Save | Passage / Threshold |
| 3 | Reveal / Seek | Relic / Object |
| 4 | Betray / Corrupt | Bond / Alliance |
| 5 | Build / Create | Threat / Danger |
| 6 | Discover / Probe | Resource / Gain |
| 7 | Flee / Avoid | Authority / Law |
| 8 | Unite / Gather | Pain / Scar |
| 9 | Command / Dominate | Dream / Illusion |
| 10 | Transform / Change | Past / Memory |
| 11 | Exchange / Negotiate | Territory / Place |
| 12 | Sacrifice / Lose | Destiny / Thread |

## TABLE C — THE PLOT TWIST

When a scene stalls and steps 1 and 2 of the restart procedure (chapter II, section 4) have not sufficed. This table injects an external event that changes the parameters of the scene without you having to invent it.

Interpret the result in service of your fiction — adapt the details to what is already in play.

| d12 | Plot twist |
|---|---|
| 1 | An expected character does not arrive. Someone else takes their place — with different intentions. |
| 2 | Information reveals that the current objective was a decoy, a misunderstanding, or one step among others. |
| 3 | A third party enters the scene with their own interests — neither ally nor enemy, but uncontrollable. |
| 4 | The environment changes abruptly. A physical or climatic element reshuffles possibilities. |
| 5 | An ally is suddenly in danger. Continue the scene or answer this call? |
| 6 | A key resource disappears, is stolen, or turns out to be unusable in this context. |
| 7 | An old acquaintance resurfaces — someone the hero had not expected to encounter here. |
| 8 | A secret of the hero is revealed, accidentally or not, to someone who was not supposed to know. |
| 9 | The adversary or obstacle changes tactics unexpectedly. What was working no longer works. |
| 10 | A brief opportunity presents itself — act now or let it pass definitively. |
| 11 | An ongoing alliance breaks. Someone chooses another side, or reveals they had no side. |
| 12 | A revelation changes the very nature of what the hero is trying to accomplish in this scene. |

## TABLE D — THE IMPROVISED NPC

To quickly generate a secondary character. Roll once per column — or only for the columns you need.

| d12 | Role | Motivation | Secret |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Merchant | Immediate survival | Carries a dangerous debt |
| 2 | Guard or soldier | Deferred vengeance | Hides an identity |
| 3 | Scholar or archivist | Thwarted love | Has witnessed a crime |
| 4 | Artisan or laborer | Quest for glory | Loyalty divided between two sides |
| 5 | Noble or official | Protection of a loved one | Conceals an illness or weakness |
| 6 | Beggar or exile | Accumulation of wealth | Had a glorious past they deny |
| 7 | Mercenary or hunter | Thirst for knowledge | Works as an informant |
| 8 | Priest, shaman, or healer | Personal redemption | Is fleeing something or someone |
| 9 | Messenger or scout | Obedience to an authority | Is an illegitimate heir |
| 10 | Criminal or smuggler | Desire for freedom | Participated in a shameful event |
| 11 | Child or apprentice | Fear of a specific consequence | Possesses a gift they ignore or hide |
| 12 | Traveler or foreigner | Uncontrollable curiosity | Lives on a foundational lie |

## TABLE E — LOCATIONS

To quickly generate the atmosphere and distinctive detail of a place. Roll once for the Ambiance, once for the Distinctive Element. Combine both into a coherent image.

| d12 | Ambiance | Distinctive element |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Abandoned | A leaning tower that still stands, inexplicably |
| 2 | Overcrowded | Ancient graffiti in a forgotten language |
| 3 | In progressive ruin | A dried-up fountain at the center of everything |
| 4 | Prosperous in appearance | Open and empty cages |
| 5 | Under latent tension | A half-erased fresco, deliberately |
| 6 | Mysterious, opaque | Traces of recent combat, without bodies |
| 7 | Sacred or protected | An overgrown garden that has reclaimed its rights |
| 8 | Forbidden or monitored | A door walled up from the inside |
| 9 | Familiar but slightly wrong | Broken lanterns replaced by candles |
| 10 | Foreign, disorienting | An improvised market where there should not be one |
| 11 | Hostile to human presence | The remains of a dead animal, never moved |
| 12 | Suspiciously peaceful | Flowers that do not bloom in this season |

## TABLE F — COMPLICATIONS

To consult when a Snag occurs (a "1" rolled on any die) and the fiction does not spontaneously suggest what goes wrong. This table describes *what derails*, not *how to fix it*.

| d12 | Complication |
|---|---|
| 1 | A third party observes the scene — and draws incorrect but dangerous conclusions. |
| 2 | Time suddenly runs short. What could wait can no longer. |
| 3 | A useful resource becomes inaccessible at the precise moment it would have been needed. |
| 4 | A noise, a gesture, or one word too many attracts unwanted attention. |
| 5 | The terrain changes — a passage closes, an exit disappears, landmarks shift. |
| 6 | An ally is put in indirect danger by the hero's actions. |
| 7 | False information begins to circulate about the hero or their intentions. |
| 8 | The adversary or obstacle receives reinforcement or an advantage that nothing foreshadowed. |
| 9 | An important object is lost, damaged, or changes hands without the hero realizing it immediately. |
| 10 | The hero is recognized — by someone they did not want to encounter here, for reasons they would rather forget. |
| 11 | The situation attracts the attention of an authority — local, institutional, or otherwise. |
| 12 | An uncomfortable truth emerges at the worst moment, about the hero or about someone they trusted. |

## TABLE G — SECRETS

To fuel the late revelations of a campaign. This table is designed to be consulted *in advance* — at the beginning of a campaign or arc — to lay hidden truths that will emerge in due time. It can also be consulted on the fly when a scene demands a revelation you had not anticipated.

| d12 | Secret |
|---|---|
| 1 | The apparent enemy is protecting someone the hero loves, or believes they are protecting. |
| 2 | A trusted ally works for an opposing faction — not out of betrayal, but out of necessity. |
| 3 | The hero's objective has already been attempted by someone else — and caused a catastrophe that nobody recognized as such. |
| 4 | One of the hero's resources has an origin they did not know — and that origin has implications. |
| 5 | Someone the hero believes dead is alive. Their survival changes everything. |
| 6 | The main antagonist acts under duress. They are not free in their choices. |
| 7 | The hero is linked to the very origin of the problem they are trying to solve — directly or by inheritance. |
| 8 | An authority figure the hero trusted has been corrupt since the beginning of the campaign. |
| 9 | The promised reward does not exist, has already changed hands, or was never what it was said to be. |
| 10 | An NPC treated as neutral or secondary is the true instigator of events. |
| 11 | The location of the final confrontation has personal meaning for the hero — a link to their past or their Threads. |
| 12 | What the hero seeks to protect is already lost. They will learn this at the least opportune moment. |

## TABLE H — SCENE ENDINGS

When a scene stretches without finding a natural conclusion. This table proposes a way to close it properly and open the next one. A scene that ends, even incompletely, is always preferable to a scene that spins its wheels.

| d12 | Scene ending |
|---|---|
| 1 | An external interruption forces departure. The scene closes before being resolved — and what was at stake remains in suspense. |
| 2 | The sought information arrives through an unexpected channel. The scene was a false lead — but not without lessons. |
| 3 | An NPC asks a question the hero cannot answer honestly. Silence settles. |
| 4 | The threat or the stake of the scene shifts — it will be addressed elsewhere, in another context. |
| 5 | A tacit agreement is found, without everything being said or resolved. Each party leaves with something. |
| 6 | The hero realizes they were looking for the wrong thing. The scene closes on this understanding. |
| 7 | A promise is made — explicitly or implicitly. It will have to be kept. Note it down. |
| 8 | The scene closes on a silence. What was not said weighs as much as what was. |
| 9 | An external event renders the scene's objective obsolete. The world has moved on without waiting. |
| 10 | The hero chooses to leave without having obtained what they wanted. It is a choice, not a failure. |
| 11 | A partial revelation opens more questions than it closes. The next scene has already begun. |
| 12 | The scene ends on a veiled threat — spoken or implied. From one side or the other. |
