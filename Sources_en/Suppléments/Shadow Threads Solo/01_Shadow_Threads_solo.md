# THE SOLITARY THREAD — SHADOWS
## Solo Supplement for Shadow Threads

---

## HOW TO USE THIS SUPPLEMENT

*The Solitary Thread — Shadows* is a solo supplement specific to the Shadow Threads setting. It requires two documents:

- **The base SRD** — for all fundamental rules.
- **Shadow Threads** — for the setting, occult maneuvers, and Fractures.

This document replaces and adapts certain tools from *The Solitary Thread* (the generic solo supplement) to tint them with the atmosphere of the Abyss. Where *The Solitary Thread* offers a neutral Oracle, this supplement offers a corrupted Oracle. Where the generic tables offer open inspiration, the tables in this document are deliberately ambiguous and unsettling.

Keep all three documents within reach.

---

# I. THE OCCULT ORACLE

In Shadow Threads, the narrative engine is not neutral. It is tainted by the Abyss — and it often seeks to push you toward your limits or your Sin.

When you ask a closed question to the universe, roll 1d12. In this world, even good news tastes of ash.

| Result | Answer | Corrupted Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 – 4 | **NO** | Reality is worse than expected. Not only does the event not occur, but a disturbing truth or a threat from the Abyss is revealed. |
| 5 – 8 | **YES, BUT…** | You get what you wanted, but the price is carnal or psychological. The Veil grants you this victory in exchange for a bloody complication or a lie. |
| 9 – 12 | **YES** | The answer is positive and favorable. However, a visceral sense of unease persists: a strange smell, a shadow that stretches too far, or the nagging feeling of being watched. |

### Adjusting probability

As in *The Solitary Thread*, assess the likelihood of a positive answer before rolling and adjust the threshold accordingly.

| Likelihood | YES threshold | YES, BUT… threshold | NO threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unlikely | 11 – 12 | 7 – 10 | 1 – 6 |
| Uncertain *(default)* | 9 – 12 | 5 – 8 | 1 – 4 |
| Likely | 6 – 12 | 4 – 5 | 1 – 3 |
| Nearly certain | 3 – 12 | 2 | 1 |

> **Note:** In Shadow Threads, the YES is never fully reassuring. Even at 9-12, the Abyss leaves a trace. Do not let a positive result make you lower your guard — the invisible Game Master of this universe is not benevolent.

# IV. INTRODUCTORY SCENARIO: THE CUT THREAD

This short scenario is designed to launch your first solo chronicle in Shadow Threads. It will test your humanity, your clock management, and your ability to use the Loom of the Fates.

## Preparation

**Recommended mode:** Normal (The Initiate).

**The starting point:** You know a certain Elias, a worldly antique dealer or pawnbroker who secretly dabbled in the resale of minor occult artifacts. He left you a terrified voicemail last night: *"They're undoing the threads... They're erasing me. I hid the spool in the..."* The message cuts off in a scream muffled by the sound of flesh tearing.

## The Scenario Clock: The Erasure (4 segments)

Create a 4-segment clock called **The Erasure**.

> Tick a segment each time you obtain a Failure (1-4) during a major action in this investigation. If the clock fills, the entity that killed Elias finishes erasing him from reality: everyone (except you) forgets his existence, his apartment becomes a vacant lot, and the thing turns toward you.

## Scene 1: Elias's Apartment (The Hook)

You arrive at Elias's place. The door is ajar, the smell of copper and ozone (Table B) seizes your throat. Inside, Elias is nothing more than a puddle of organic matter partially fused with the carpet and the floorboards. His eyes, intact, still roll in this magma of flesh.

**Suggested action:** Perform the *Probe the Veil* maneuver. You are looking for what Elias hid — the famous "spool."

- In case of success with a cost (5-8), you find the object but suffer a Light Mental Wound or the thing spots you.
- **The Object:** If you succeed, you find an actual sewing spool — but the thread is made of braided human nerves and vibrates with occult energy.

## Scene 2: Follow the Thread (Exploration)

The nerve spool "pulls" in a specific direction, toward a location in the city.

**Use the Oracle:** Ask a question to define the location. E.g.: *"Does this lead toward the old port warehouses?"* Roll 1d12.

**The Obstacle — The Fracture:** On the way, reality distorts. Passersby suddenly have no faces and space stretches. You must *Brave the Danger* (Environmental Obstacle — Threat Level: Serious Mental Wound).

> This is a good moment to justify using your Clotho Thread (Conviction) — spend a token to add +1d12 to your roll and keep your mind clear against this distortion.

## Scene 3: The Flesh Weaver (The Confrontation)

You arrive at the source. The place is a liminal space, decorated with human skins stretched like canvases. At the center stands **The Weaver** — an Amalgam: several workers' bodies fused around a rusty industrial sewing machine. It uses the threads ripped from Elias to fabricate an unholy garment.

**Resolution:** You have a choice of approach.

- *Engage in Conflict:* You attempt to destroy the Weaver through force or magic. Threat: Serious Physical Wound.
- *Negotiate with the Invisible:* You try to trade Elias's nerve spool for something else, or offer one of your Resources — or even some of your own blood.

> If you choose to attack cruelly or bargain ignobly, you can invoke your Atropos Thread (Sin) to regain 1 token — adding +1d4 (penalty) to your roll.

## Scene 4: Epilogue and Consequences

Once the scene is resolved — victory, flight, or pact — the clock stops.

**If you saved the spool:** It becomes a Cursed Resource. It can guide you toward anyone, but each use inflicts a Light Wound on you.

**Advancement:** The experience has changed you. Write a summary sentence in your Thread Journal and check whether this ordeal justifies increasing one of your traits — or transforming a Behavior trait to reflect your new cynicism.
