# V. Solo Tools of the Shadow

Playing solo in **Shadow Threads** means letting the Veil lie to you and abuse you. The narrative engine is not neutral: it is tainted by the Abyss and often seeks to push you to your limits or towards your Sin (Atropos).

### 1. The Occult Oracle (d12)
When you ask a closed question to the universe, roll **1d12**. In this world, even good news tastes like ash.

| Result | Answer | Corrupted Interpretation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 – 4** | **NO** | The reality is worse than expected. Not only does the event not occur, but a disturbing truth or a threat from the Abyss is revealed. |
| **5 – 8** | **YES, BUT...** | You get what you wanted, but the price is carnal or psychological. The Veil grants you this victory in exchange for a bloody complication or a lie. |
| **9 – 12** | **YES** | The answer is positive and favorable. However, a feeling of visceral unease persists: a strange smell, a shadow that stretches too far, or the lingering feeling of being watched. |

### 3. Urban Investigation Generator
To start a new session or narrative arc, roll 1d6 on the following three columns to weave the thread of your investigation.

#### A. The Trigger (The victim or the incident)
1. The atrociously mutilated corpse of a former colleague or loved one.
2. A child or homeless person frantically drawing future events with their own blood.
3. A cursed artifact delivered to your home by mistake (or malice).
4. A series of inexplicable suicides in a corporate building of glass and steel.
5. A liminal ghost/echo begging you to find its body before it is "consumed".
6. An area of your neighborhood where people forget their own names upon entering.

#### B. The Occult Secret (The real stakes)
1. A synthetic drug made from memories of agony.
2. A "soul surgery" ritual aimed at separating humanity from the flesh.
3. The imminent opening of a Liminal Door under the foundations of the city.
4. An ancient text hidden in the algorithms of a social network, driving its readers mad.
5. The theft of a "Thread of Destiny", retroactively altering a public figure's past.
6. An entity that reproduces by implanting itself in the psychological traumas of its victims.

#### C. The Involved Faction (The adversaries)
1. The Necromantic Underworld (Traffickers of magic-infused organs).
2. The Ordo Puritas (Fanatical inquisitors ready to burn a neighborhood to kill a demon).
3. The Architects of Flesh (A cult of underground surgeons and body artists).
4. The Glass Council (Demons in tailored suits running powerful multinationals).
5. The Forgotten Swarm (A collective intelligence formed by possessed outcasts and marginalized people).
6. A rival Initiate, convinced that their survival depends on your death or sacrifice.
