# IV. The Fractures of the City

In the universe of **Shadow Threads**, adversaries are not simple monsters listed in a bestiary. They are "Fractures", errors in reality, entities from the Abyss, or humans whose flesh and soul have been corrupted by the Veil.

### 1. Generating a Fracture on the Fly
To create an unpredictable threat, roll (or choose) an Origin, a Motivation, and a Twist.

**A. The Origin (1d6)**

1. **The Hollowed Human:** A mundane person whose mind has been replaced by an entity.
2. **The Corrupted Swarm:** A swarm of urban animals acting with a single, cruel intelligence.
3. **The Haunted Object / The Artifact:** A place or inanimate object that bends reality around it.
4. **The Thread-Eater:** An invisible predator that feeds on the threads of destiny.
5. **The Amalgam:** A hideous fusion of several bodies or of flesh and urban materials (metal, concrete, glass).
6. **The Liminal Echo:** The projection of an extreme emotion or past trauma that takes physical form.

**B. The Dark Motivation (1d6)**

1. **Devour Identity:** Wants to steal your memories or take your place in the mundane world.
2. **Propagate the Rift:** Seeks to tear the Veil a little more to let its masters through.
3. **Impose its Pain:** No longer feels anything and uses the suffering of others as a substitute.
4. **Protect a Secret:** A guardian placed there by an occult cult or faction.
5. **Collect:** Seeks to appropriate an organ, an emotion, or a specific Resource from the Initiate.
6. **Corrupt Innocence:** Specifically targets what connects you to your humanity (your Conviction).

**C. The Twist (The touch of visceral horror) (1d6)**

1. Has eyes, mouths, or teeth under the skin, in unlikely places.
2. Bleeds black sap, acid, or cold ashes.
3. Speaks with the voice of your deceased loved ones or uses your own regrets against you.
4. Gives off a heady odor (ozone, rotten meat, child's perfume) that causes nausea (penalty).
5. Moves jerkily, with too many joints, producing a sound of broken bones.
6. Absorbs the light around it, creating a zone of freezing cold and dense shadows.

### 2. Examples of Threats by Level

#### REALISTIC Mode (Human Horror and the Unknown)

* **The New Blood Cult (Opposition Group):** Hypnotized ordinary citizens. 
    * *Threat Level:* Physical Light Wound, but inflicted in numbers.
    * *Complication:* Striking innocents attacks your Clotho's Thread (Conviction).
* **The Architect Killer (Determined Antagonist):** A serial killer who uses his murders to draw runes in the city.
    * *Threat Level:* Serious Wound (Physical).
    * *Complication:* He targets an element of your mundane life if you fail.

#### NORMAL Mode (Nightmares from the Abyss)

* **The Name Devourer (Liminal Entity):** A shadow that feeds on identity.
    * *Threat Level:* Serious Wound (Mental).
    * *Complication:* In case of failure, you temporarily lose access to one of your Resources (a contact forgets you, an object loses its function).
* **The Flesh Rift (Environmental Obstacle):** An apartment whose walls pulsate and secrete acid.
    * *Threat Level:* Serious Wound (Physical and Mental).
    * *Complication:* A "Success with a cost" closes the rift but permeates the character with an odor of sulfur that will alert other entities.

#### HEROIC Mode (Lords of Shadow)

* **The Flesh Angel (Major Antagonist):** An immense, magnificent, and terrifying being, made of merged bodies in prayer.
    * *Threat Level:* Mortal Wound (Mental and Physical).
    * *Complication:* Directly attacks Lachesis's Thread (Destiny). A success with a cost forces the Initiate to sacrifice a Trait to avoid being assimilated by the Angel.
* **The Awakening of the District (Timed Emergency):** A ritual that threatens to tip a whole city block into the Abyss.
    * *Threat Level:* Mortal Wound. 
    * *Complication:* Each failed roll or roll with a cost accelerates the Clock. If it fills up, the district is lost, and the Veil is permanently torn in this area.
