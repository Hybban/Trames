# VI. Introductory Scenario: The Cut Thread

This short scenario is designed to launch your first solo chronicle in **Shadow Threads**. It will test your humanity, your management of clocks, and your ability to use the Loom of the Fates.

### Preparation

* **Recommended mode:** Normal (The Initiate).
* **Starting point:** You know a certain Elias, an antiquarian or mundane pawnbroker who secretly dabbled in the resale of minor occult artifacts. He left you a terrified voicemail last night: *"They are undoing the threads... They are erasing me. I hid the spool in the..."* The message cuts off in a scream muffled by the sound of tearing flesh.

### Scenario Clock: The Erasure (4 Segments)
Create a 4-segment clock called **The Erasure**. 

* *Rule:* Check a segment every time you get a **Failure (1-4)** during a major action in this investigation. If the clock fills up, the entity that killed Elias finishes crossing him out of reality: everyone (except you) forgets his existence, his apartment becomes a vacant lot, and the thing turns towards you.

---

### Scene 1: Elias's Apartment (The Hook)
You arrive at Elias's place. The door is ajar, the smell of copper and ozone (Table B) catches your throat. Inside, Elias is nothing more than a puddle of organic matter partially fused with the rug and the floorboards. His eyes, intact, still roll in this magma of flesh.

* **Suggested action:** Perform the maneuver **Probe the Veil**. You are looking for what Elias hid (the famous "spool"). 
    * *Reminder:* In case of a success with a cost (5-8), you find the object but suffer a **Light Mental Wound** or the thing spots you.
    * *The Object:* If you succeed, you find a real spool of sewing thread, but the thread is made of braided human nerves and vibrates with occult energy. 

### Scene 2: Follow the Thread (Exploration)
The nerve spool "pulls" in a specific direction, towards a place in the city. 

* **Use the Oracle:** Ask a question to define the location (e.g., "Does it lead to the old port warehouses?"). Roll 1d12.
* **The Obstacle (The Rift):** On the way, reality distorts. Passersby suddenly have no faces and space stretches out. You must **Brave the Danger** (Environmental Obstacle - Threat Level: Serious Mental Wound). 
    * *The Loom of the Fates:* This is a good time to justify using your **Clotho's Thread (Conviction)** to spend a token and add +1d12 to your roll, in order to keep a clear mind in the face of this distortion.

### Scene 3: The Flesh Weaver (Confrontation)
You arrive at the source. The place is a liminal space, decorated with human skins stretched out like canvases. In the center is "The Weaver", an *Amalgam* (several workers' bodies fused around a rusty industrial sewing machine). It uses the threads torn from Elias to make an unholy garment.

* **Resolution:** You have the choice of approach:
    * **Engage in Conflict:** You attempt to destroy the Weaver by force or magic (Threat: Serious Physical Wound).
    * **Negotiate with the Invisible:** You try to trade Elias's nerve spool for something else, or offer one of your **Resources** (or even a little of your own blood).
    * *The Loom of the Fates:* If you choose to attack it cruelly or bargain despicably, you can invoke your **Atropos's Thread (Sin)** to regain 1 token, by adding **+1d4** (penalty) to your roll.

### Scene 4: Epilogue and Consequences
Once the scene is resolved (victory, escape, or pact), the clock stops. 

* **If you saved the spool:** It becomes a **Cursed Resource**. It can guide you to anyone, but each use inflicts a Light Wound.
* **Evolution:** The experience has changed you. Write a summary sentence in your Logbook and check if this ordeal justifies increasing one of your traits (or transforming a Behavior trait to reflect your new cynicism).
