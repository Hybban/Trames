# IV. Maneuvers and Game Actions

The Moirai do not dictate every move, but they weave moments of tension. Maneuvers are knots in the fabric—the moments when your thread crosses that of an obstacle, an opponent, or the world. Each maneuver has two sides: a fixed mechanic that always applies, and a narrative flavor that belongs to your story. The first is a rule. The second is an invitation.

Of course, there are many other possible actions. Those presented here are the ones that define most possible interactions. They serve as a foundation for creating many new situations.

## 1. Fundamental Maneuvers

* **Tighten the Thread (confront an immediate threat):**
    * **9-12:** The obstacle is overcome. Increase a used trait by one level.
    * **5-8:** You succeed, but a trait decreases by one level or you receive a Frayed Thread.
    * **1-4:** The threat hits you full force. Immediate consequences.
* **Reading the Plot (analyzing, observing a situation or a character):**
    * **9-12:** Clear information. The Storyteller must answer a question honestly.
    * **5-8:** The information is found, but it takes time or draws attention.
    * **1-4:** Wrong conclusion or a trap is set.
* **Cut the Thread (Assert yourself through force, magic, or rhetoric.) :** 
    * **9-12:** The opponent is repelled or defeated.
    * **5-8:** Bitter victory; one trait is reduced by one level, and you receive a Frayed Thread.
    * **1-4:** The opponent gains the upper hand (serious injury or worse).
* **Weaving a New Thread (taking action, using resources):** To obtain help or use rare equipment.
    * **9–12:** The resource is used perfectly and remains available.
    * **5–8:** The objective is achieved, but the resource is depleted or temporarily inaccessible.
    * **1-4:** The resource fails or backfires.

## 2. Adversity and Timers
Some complex challenges are represented by **Timers** (circles with 4 or 6 segments).

* **Timed Urgency:** Each failure or successful attempt with a cost fills one segment. If the clock is full, the maximum threat is triggered.
* **Threat Levels:** An obstacle inflicts damage based on its nature
    * Minor: a group of NPCs with no stakes.
    * Serious: a rival or a significant danger. 
    * Deadly: an extreme danger. 

Each level also corresponds to a number of spaces to fill on a clock in the case of the opposing objective’s progression. 

* A group of NPCs with no stakes: 2 spaces
* A rival: 4 spaces
* An extreme danger: 6 spaces

### 3. EXAMPLES OF THE GAME IN ACTION

#### Example 1: The Cult of the Shadow (Linen Thread - 1920s)

*In this realistic and punishing mode, every injury counts and the tension is palpable. Players have only 1 Frayed Thread (Minor Injury) space.*

**The Context:** Arthur (Veteran), Beatrice (Medium), and Charles (Scholar) have broken into the mansion of a wealthy London industrialist to investigate an unexplained death.
**The Obstacle:** The Game Master sets up a Timed Emergency: *The Arrival of Scotland Yard* (4-segment clock).

* **The Action:** Beatrice attempts to analyze the body covered in symbols using the **Read the Pattern** maneuver and her *Medium* trait. She rolls her dice and gets a **7 (Success with a cost)**.
* **The Resolution:** The Game Master gives her a crucial clue about the nature of the cult. However, the price to pay is twofold: the horrific vision inflicts a mental wound on her (1 *Frayed Thread* box checked), and the investigation takes time, which checks **1 segment** on the *Scotland Yard Arrival* clock.
* **The Impact of the Linen Thread:** Since Beatrice has only one *Frayed Thread* box in this mode, her minor injury gauge is already full. At the next mental or physical shock, she will immediately switch to *Taut Thread* and suffer the +1d4 penalty!

#### Example 2: Race to Asteroid X-89 (Woolen Thread - Sci-Fi)

*The standard game mode. The heroes are competent and take hits normally (3 squares of *Frayed Thread*).*

**The Context:** Kael (Pilot), Jaxx (Engineer), and Vex (Gunner) are space pirates. They’re racing toward a mineral-rich asteroid, but a corporate cruiser is hot on their heels.
**The Timers:** The Game Master sets two opposing timers. *Reach the Asteroid* (Players’ Objective: 4 segments) and *Cruiser Interception* (Threat: 6 segments).

* **Action 1:** Kael wants to weave through a field of magnetic debris to buy time. He makes a **Walk a Tightrope** roll and gets a **10 (Total Success)**.
* **Resolution 1:** Perfect maneuver. Kael increases his *Master Pilot* trait by one level (until the end of the scene), and the Game Master marks **1 segment** on the *Reach the Asteroid* timer.
* **Action 2:** Jaxx attempts to overload the engines with **Weave a New Thread** using her *Illegal Plasma Generator* resource (which allows her to roll two dice). She rolls a **6 (Success with a Price)**, but one of her dice shows a **1 (The Glitch)**.
* **Resolution 2:** The action succeeds: the ship lurches forward, marking a **2nd segment** on their objective. But the cost applies (Jaxx marks 1 *Frayed Thread* due to overheating in the engine room) AND the Glitch triggers: the generator blows. The *Illegal Plasma Generator* resource becomes unusable for the rest of the scene.



#### Example 3: The Tomb Guardian (Silken Thread - Heroic Fantasy)

*In this epic mode, the heroes are legends in the making. They can take a lot of damage (5 spaces of Frayed Thread) before feeling the pain.*

**The Context:** Thorne (Warrior), Elara (Mage), and Lyra (Thief) face an Obsidian Golem in a booby-trapped room.
**The Challenge:** The Golem is a formidable opponent. The Game Master sets its Threat Level to **Deadly** (which is equivalent to a monster requiring 6 clock spaces to be defeated).

* **Action 1:** Thorne charges the monster head-on using **Cut the Thread**. He rolls an **8 (Success with a cost)**.
* **Resolution 1:** Thorne lands a powerful blow (the Game Master marks **1 space** on the Golem’s clock). The cost: the monster strikes back hard. Thorne marks **2 Frayed Thread boxes** because the opponent is formidable.
* **The Impact of the Silk Thread:** Despite the violence of the blow, Thorne has 5 light wound boxes. He smiles, spits out a little blood, and remains mechanically at 100% of his abilities, illustrating the pulp and heroic aspect of this mode.
* **Action 2:** Elara casts a frost spell, but rolls a **3 (Failure)**.
* **Resolution 2:** The spell bounces off the obsidian. The Golem hurls shards of rock. Since the threat is *Deadly*, the Leader doesn’t hold back: Elara immediately suffers a **Tightrope** (Serious Injury). She must now add +1d4 to her rolls, representing the pain of the stone shard lodged in her shoulder.
