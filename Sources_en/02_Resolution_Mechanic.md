# II. Resolution Mechanic

The THREADS system relies on rolling a dice pool and directly reading the highest result. This system handles success, failure, and the wear and tear of your abilities through the physical degradation of the dice.

## 1. The Dice Roll
When an action is uncertain or risky, you must make a test:

* **Selection:** Choose between **1 and 3 traits** relevant to the action.
* **Roll:** Roll the dice corresponding to the current value of these traits (d6, d8, d10, or d12).
* **Result:** Keep only the value of the **highest die**.

**Note:** The more traits you use, the more you increase your chances of success, but you also expose yourself to more complications.

## 2. Interpretation and Consequences
The score obtained on the kept die determines the outcome of the action and the evolution of your traits for the rest of the scene:

| Result | Outcome | Consequence on Traits |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **9 – 12** | **Complete Success** | The action is a full success. **Increase** the value of one of the used traits by one step. |
| **5 – 8** | **Success at a Cost** | The action succeeds, but with a consequence. **Decrease** the value of only one of the used traits by one step. |
| **1 – 4** | **Failure** | The action fails. **Decrease** the value of one of the used traits by one step. |

**Reminder:** A trait cannot exceed its maximum value. Moreover, a trait normally cannot drop below d6.

If you have no choice but to decrease a trait that is already at its minimum, that trait may become impossible to use for the remainder of the scene.

> **The Rule of "1" (The Grain of Sand):** Regardless of the final result, each "1" obtained across all rolled dice causes an additional complication. This can be a decrease of one step on an additional die, a resource becoming inaccessible, or a narrative constraint.

## 3. Degradation and Exhaustion
The value of a trait fluctuates during the game. At the end of a scene, all traits return to their initial value (their maximum defined at creation).

* **The Archetype:** This trait is the core of your identity; it can **never drop below d6**.
* **Other Traits:** If a trait (Behavior or Ability) must drop below d6, it becomes **unusable** for the rest of the scene. It cannot be used again until after a period of rest or a change in narrative context.
* **Other cases:** If a trait must decrease and you do not wish to lower it (or cannot): you may also sacrifice a resource or take a light wound instead. Similarly, if you were to increase a trait and it has reached its maximum, the player may heal one wound box instead. Use these flexibilities wisely to serve the story.
