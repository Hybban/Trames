# III. Health and Wounds

In THREADS, health is not limited to the physical. It represents your overall integrity: your body, your mind, and your social reputation. These three aspects are managed on a single damage track composed of three independent tiers.

## 1. Damage Tiers
Damage accumulates according to its severity. As long as the damage is light, there is no mechanical impact, but it acts as a buffer before more critical states.

* **Light Wounds (3 boxes):** No immediate mechanical impact. If you take damage while these boxes are full, it spills over into serious damage.
* **Serious Wounds (3 boxes):** You must add **1d4** to all your dice rolls.
* **Mortal Wounds (3 boxes):** The situation is critical. You must add **3d4** to all your dice rolls.

> **The "Dead Weight" Effect:** The d4s added by wounds are considered trait dice rolled for the action. They cannot roll higher than a 4 and thus provide no possibility of success, even partial. Their main purpose is to increase the probability of rolling 1s, thereby triggering complications or the degradation of your resources. They mechanically represent the character's fatigue and pain.

## 2. Taking Damage
Damage is often taken as a result of a dice roll:

* **Complete Success (9 – 12):** The character avoids the worst. Damage is non-existent, or perhaps light in some very critical situations.
* **Success at a Cost (5 – 8):** The character potentially takes damage. This can be offset by narrative complications instead of damage. If no narrative complication is desired, then the character takes light damage. But it can be more severe if the situation calls for it.
* **Failure (1 – 4):** The character is fully hit. The consequences are severe and immediate. Depending on the context, this could be physical, psychological, or social damage.

## 3. Recovery and Healing
The time and resources required to heal depend on the depth of the injuries:

* **Light Recovery:** All light wound boxes are automatically cleared at the **end of the scene**.
* **Deep Recovery:** To clear **Serious** or **Mortal** wound boxes, a simple pause is not enough. The hero must engage in a dedicated resting scene and justify adequate support (medical care, therapy, a sanctuary, or help from a specialized ally).

### Level of Heroism and Realism
Depending on the desired level of heroism for the campaign, the Game Master may modify the damage tiers as well as the way they can be healed. Here are a few examples of possible modifications:

| Level | Light boxes | Serious boxes | Mortal boxes | Light Recovery | Deep Recovery |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Realistic** | 1 | 2 | 2 | End of scene | 1 resting scene + Help  |
| **Normal** | 3 | 3 | 3 | End of scene | 1 resting scene |
| **Heroic** | 5 | 4 | 3 | Immediate (post-conflict) | Simple resting scene |
| **Legendary** | 6 | 6 | 4 | Immediate (post-roll) | Automatic (end of scene) |
