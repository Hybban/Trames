# V. Evolution and Advancement

In THREADS, evolution is purely narrative: it does not depend on calculations, but on the impact of events on the story.

## 1. Improvement through Deeds
A hero does not progress by accumulating experience. They progress because the Moirai have noticed that their thread has changed. This happens when an **arc knot** is reached — a moment when the story turns, and the hero emerges from it changed.
An arc knot is recognized when at least one of these conditions is met:

* Lachesis's Thread has been honored: the hero has accomplished a significant step towards their Destiny, or has seen it transform before their eyes.
* Clotho's Thread has been tested: the hero's Conviction has been put to the test, whether they held to it or not. The important thing is that they had to choose.
* Atropos's Thread has been pulled: the hero has given in to their Sin and suffered the consequences, or conversely resisted it at a critical moment.

The Game Master validates the arc knot in agreement with the table. It is not an automatic reward — it is a collective recognition that something important has just been woven. If so, the hero can increase the maximum value of one of their traits by one step. The weave of their destiny becomes stronger, with a more complex and beautiful shape. They can also create a new trait. If they create one, it generally starts at the **d6** value.

### Trait Levels
* **d6:** Discovery. The hero is beginning to develop a skill or aptitude.
* **d8:** Mastery. The hero is competent and reliable in this area.
* **d10:** Expertise. The hero is recognized for their high level of competence.
* **d12:** Perfection. The hero reaches the pinnacle of their art. This is the highest level of mastery possible and must be justified by the story. These are the strongest and most complex threads of the hero's weave.

* **New Traits:** The story may justify the appearance of a new trait (learning, new title). It generally starts at the value **d6**. Threads are added to the hero's weave, reinforcing its complexity and beauty.

### Arc Thresholds
A campaign is not a uniform succession of **arc knots**. It has a rhythm — moments when the story turns, when the stakes change nature, when what was true at the beginning is no longer quite so. These moments are the **arc thresholds**. The Game Master does not need to plan them in advance. She recognizes them when they arrive, by observing the evolution of the Threads at the table.

**First third of the campaign — The World Reacts**

The heroes have acted. The world has taken note. A previously passive force comes into play — a faction, a rival, a threat that the heroes' actions have awakened. Introduce a new short clock for this force, and start advancing it. The resolution of each clock could lead to progression.

**Mid-campaign — The Turning Point**

Something the heroes thought was true turns out to be false, incomplete, or more complex than expected. A Thread changes shape without disappearing. The Destiny of one of the heroes can be reformulated — not erased, but seen from another angle. It is often here that the first Burnt Threads become a real possibility. And above all, new opportunities to connect the different Threads of all the heroes with the threads of other characters.

**Last third — The Price Becomes Visible**

The campaign enters its final phase. The stakes are clear, resources depleted, Threads stretched to the breaking point. The Game Master toughens the obstacles — not to punish the players, but because the Moirai never offer their endings without demanding something in return. Clocks can be used to represent the evolution of the campaign, enemy objectives, the consequences of the players' actions...

> **Note:** Arc knots are not mechanical milestones — they have no precise trigger. They are narrative signals that the Game Master reads in the state of the table: how the Threads evolve, how many knots have been reached, what the general tone of recent scenes is. A short campaign can cross its three thresholds in three sessions. An epic can stretch them over months.

## 2. Narrative Flexibility

* **Reweaving the Thread:** At any time, if a trait no longer matches the hero's reality, you can transform (rename) it while keeping its current die value.
* **A Broken Thread:** A trauma can lock a trait at the **d6** value as long as the cause persists.
* **Removing a Thread:** If a trait completely loses its meaning in the story, it can simply be removed.

> **Final note:** Favor consistency, flexibility, and narrative impact over a rigid leveling system. The hero must bear the marks of their journey.

## 3. Campaign Endings
A THREADS campaign does not end when the players have had enough — it ends when the Moirai have finished weaving. Three types of endings are possible. They are not mutually exclusive, and a campaign can combine several depending on the heroes who traverse it.

### Destiny Fulfilled
*Lachesis has measured — the thread was exactly the right length.*

The hero has reached what they were destined for. Their Lachesis Thread has been fulfilled, in its original form or in the form it took at the mid-campaign turning point. It is an ending of victory — but not necessarily a happy ending. A fulfilled destiny can be costly, and the Moirai owe nothing to the heroes who make it to the end.

A player whose hero experiences this ending can choose to continue with them in a new campaign. Their Arc starts from zero, their Destiny is rewritten — but their traits, resources, and their Clotho and Atropos Threads bear the marks of what they have been through.

### Sin Victorious
*Atropos has cut — the thread was not what we thought it was.*

The Clock of Sin has filled one time too many, or the hero has made a final choice that places their Sin above everything else. This is not necessarily death — it is transformation. The hero is no longer the same moral character as at the beginning of the campaign. This ending is valid, and can be beautiful. Some of the best stories end this way.

If the player chooses to continue with this hero, their Atropos Thread must be rewritten to reflect what they have become — and their Conviction will have to answer for this change.

### The Final Burnt Thread
*Clotho has spun to the very end — even when there was nothing left to give.*

The hero has survived the impossible by sacrificing a part of themselves. This ending activates the Burnt Thread rule one last time, in the context of a conclusion. What is burned here is definitive — not just for the scene, but for the entire story. The Moirai have demanded the maximum price. It has been paid.

> **Note:** A campaign can end without all heroes experiencing the same ending. One fulfills their Destiny while another gives in to their Sin. It is a weave, not a collective result — and the Moirai weave each thread in their own way.
