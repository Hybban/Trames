# V. Evolution and Advancement

In THREADS, evolution is purely narrative: it does not depend on calculations, but on the impact of events on the story.

## 1. Improvement through Deeds
When a hero reaches a **narrative milestone** (end of an arc, accomplishment of a personal goal), they can increase the maximum value of one of their traits by one step.

* The **d12** is the pinnacle of mastery and must be justified by the story.
* **New Traits:** The story may justify the appearance of a new trait (learning, new title). It generally starts at the value **d6**.

## 2. Narrative Flexibility

* **Rewriting:** At any time, if a trait no longer matches the hero's reality, you can transform (rename) it while keeping its current die value.
* **Lasting Weakness:** A trauma can lock a trait at the **d6** value as long as the cause persists.
* **Removal:** If a trait completely loses its meaning in the story, it can simply be removed.

> **Final note:** Favor consistency, flexibility, and narrative impact over a rigid leveling system. The hero must bear the marks of their journey.
