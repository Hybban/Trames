# V. Evolution and Progression

In TRAMES, evolution is purely narrative: it does not depend on calculations, but on the impact of events on the story.

## 1. Improvement Through Feats of Arms
A hero does not progress by accumulating experience. He progresses because the Moirai have noticed that his thread has changed. This occurs when an **arc node** is reached—a pivotal moment where the story shifts, and the hero emerges changed.
An arc node is recognized when at least one of these conditions is met:

* The Thread of Lachesis has been honored: the hero has taken a significant step toward their Destiny, or has seen it transform before their eyes.
* The Thread of Clotho has been tested: the hero’s Conviction has been put to the test, whether they upheld it or not. What matters is that they had to choose.
* The Thread of Atropos has been drawn: the hero has succumbed to their Sin and suffered the consequences, or, conversely, resisted it at a critical moment.

The Game Master validates the arc node according to the table. This is not an automatic reward; it is a collective acknowledgment that something important has just been woven. If so, the player may increase the maximum value of one of her traits by one level. The fabric of her destiny becomes stronger, taking on a more complex and beautiful form. She may also create a new trait. If she creates one, it generally starts at a value of **d6**.

### Trait Levels
* **d6:** Discovery. The hero begins to develop a skill or ability.
* **d8:** Mastery. The hero is competent and reliable in this area.
* **d10:** Expertise. The hero is recognized for their high level of skill.
* **d12:** Perfection. The hero reaches the pinnacle of their craft. This is the highest possible level of mastery and must be justified by the story. These are the strongest and most complex threads of the hero’s tapestry.

* **New Traits:** The story may justify the emergence of a new trait (learning, new title). It generally starts at a **d6** value. Strands are added to the hero’s tapestry, reinforcing its complexity and beauty.

### Arc Thresholds
A campaign is not a uniform succession of **arc nodes**. It has a rhythm, moments when the story shifts, when the stakes change in nature, when what was true at the beginning is no longer quite true. These moments are the **arc thresholds**. The Game Master doesn’t need to plan them in advance. She recognizes them when they arrive, by observing the evolution of the Threads at the table.

**First Third of the Campaign — The World Reacts**

The heroes have acted. The world has taken note. A previously passive force enters the fray—a faction, a rival, a threat that the heroes’ actions have awakened. Introduce a new short timer for this force, and begin advancing it. The resolution of each timer may result in a progression.

**Mid-campaign — The Turning Point**

Something the heroes believed to be true turns out to be false, incomplete, or more complex than expected. A Thread changes form without disappearing. The Destiny of one of the heroes may be reframed—not erased, but seen from a different angle. It is often here that the first Burnt Plots become a real possibility. And above all, new opportunities arise to connect the various Threads of all the heroes with the threads of the other characters.

**Final Third — The Price Becomes Visible**

The campaign enters its final phase. The stakes are clear, resources are depleted, and the Threads are stretched to the limit. The Game Master makes the obstacles more difficult, not to punish the players, but because the Fates never offer their resolutions without demanding something in return. Clocks can be used to represent the campaign’s progression, the adversary’s objectives, the consequences of the players’ actions... 

> **Note:** Arc nodes are not mechanical milestones; they have no specific trigger. They are narrative signals that the Game Master reads from the state of the table: how the Strands are evolving, how many nodes have been reached, what the general tone of recent scenes is. A short campaign can pass through its three thresholds in three sessions. An epic can stretch them out over months.

## 2. Narrative Flexibility

* **Re-weaving the Fabric:** At any time, if a trait no longer fits the hero’s reality, you can transform it (rename it) while retaining its current die value.
* **A Broken Thread:** A trauma can lock a trait at a value of **d6** as long as the cause persists.
* **Removing a Thread:** If a trait loses all meaning within the story, it can simply be removed.

> **Final Note:** Prioritize consistency, flexibility, and narrative impact over a rigid leveling system. The hero must bear the marks of their journey.

## 3. Campaign Endings
A TRAMES campaign does not end when the players have had enough—it ends when the Moirai have finished weaving. Three types of endings are possible. They are not mutually exclusive, and a campaign may combine several depending on the heroes who journey through it.

### Destiny Fulfilled
*Lachesis has measured; the thread was exactly the right length.*

The hero has achieved what they were destined for. Their Thread of Lachesis has been fulfilled, either in its original form or in the form it took at the mid-campaign turning point. This is a victorious ending, but not necessarily a happy one. A fulfilled destiny can come at a high cost, and the Moirai owe nothing to the heroes who reach the end.

A player whose hero meets this end may choose to continue with him in a new campaign. His Arc starts anew, his Destiny is rewritten, but his traits, resources, and the Threads of Clotho and Atropos bear the marks of what he has endured.

### The Victorious Sin
*Atropos has cut; the thread was not what we thought.*

The Clock of Sin has filled one time too many, or the hero has made a final choice that places their Sin above all else. This is not necessarily death; it is transformation. The hero is no longer the same moral character as at the start of the campaign. This ending is valid, and can be beautiful. Some of the best stories end this way.

If the player chooses to continue with this hero, their Thread of Atropos must be rewritten to reflect what they have become, and their Conviction must account for this change.

### The Final Burnt Thread
*Clotho spun to the very end; even when there was nothing left to give.*

The hero survived the impossible by sacrificing a part of himself. This ending triggers the Burnt Fate rule one last time, in the context of conclusion. What is burned here is final, not just for the scene, but for the entire story. The Fates demanded the highest price. It has been paid.

> **Note:** A campaign may end without all heroes sharing the same fate. One fulfills their Destiny while another succumbs to their Sin. This is a thread, not a collective outcome, and the Fates weave each thread in their own way.
