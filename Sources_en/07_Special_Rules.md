# VII. Special Rules: The Threads of Destiny

These optional mechanics reinforce the dramatic and thematic aspect of the system. They introduce a management of luck and sacrifice at the heart of your story.

---

## 1. Ariadne's Thread (The Exchange of Luck)
Ariadne's Thread represents a pool of shared luck. It is a resource that circulates between the players and adversity.

#### A. In a Group (Flow of Tokens)
*   **The Pool:** Place a number of tokens in the center of the table equal to the **number of players + 1**.
*   **Player Usage:** After a dice roll, if you are not satisfied with the result, you can spend a token to add **1d12** to the highest result already obtained. Once used, the token is given to the Game Master (GM).
    * Depending on the game mode (Realistic, Normal, Heroic, Legendary), the die you add is not necessarily a d12 but can be different. (XXX Make a correspondence table of the dice to use)
*   **GM Usage:** The GM can spend these tokens to reinforce an obstacle (tick boxes on a clock, increase the type of damage dealt, etc.) or force the degradation of an additional trait after a failure. Once used by the GM, the token returns to the center of the table.

#### B. Solo (Cycles of Destiny)
*   **The Pool:** You start the game with **2 tokens** of Ariadne's Thread.
*   **Usage:** Spend a token to add **1d12** to a crucial roll. The token is then "committed" (flip it or move it).
*   **Recovery (The Twist):** To recover your committed tokens, you must accept a narrative defeat. During a risky action, do not roll the dice: declare an automatic failure, suffer the normal damage, and recover all your tokens.

---

## 2. The Burnt Thread (The Ultimate Sacrifice)
The Burnt Thread is a tragic survival rule designed primarily for solo play, to prevent a premature death from breaking the narrative arc.

*   **Activation:** Usable only when facing a fatal outcome (final mortal wound or failure of an end-of-arc objective).
*   **Effect:** Your action is instantly transformed into a **Total Success (9-12)**. The danger is averted or the enemy defeated.
*   **The Price:** You must **permanently erase** one of your traits from your sheet. This trait is "burnt": it can no longer be used, rewritten or increased. 
    *   *Note on the Archetype:* If you burn your Archetype, your character survives but loses their essence. You must create a new Archetype with a score of **d6**, representing a broken hero who must rebuild themselves.

It should be possible to use it in group mode to avoid the tragic death of a character. In this case, the players will have to decide together which trait to sacrifice, and this sacrifice must have significant consequences on the rest of the story.
