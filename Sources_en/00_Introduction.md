# I. Introduction

THREADS is a narrative role-playing system that incorporates mechanical elements from more traditional role-playing games. It is designed to facilitate the quick setup of campaigns and to allow for great flexibility in interpretation. In THREADS, a hero’s identity is defined by a set of free traits that can evolve as the story unfolds.

**On the use of genders:** Around the gaming table, whether virtual or physical, there are people of all possible genders. But since they are people and that word is feminine, we will therefore have female players at the table. In fiction, we have characters, and that word is masculine. So, regardless of their gender, the characters will be heroes. To put it simply, when we talk about real people, we use the feminine form. When we talk about fictional characters, we use the masculine form. And yes, the game master is a player just like the others.

**Emotional Safety:**  Role-playing is first and foremost a leisure activity, a pastime. The goal is to have a good time with friends. There is therefore no reason for anyone to feel uncomfortable or unsafe. That’s why we recommend establishing safety rules for your table. These rules should be set at the start of each session and reviewed at the beginning of every campaign. There are many tools available online to help you implement these rules, such as the X-card, boundaries, and veils, among others.

**Inspirations:** Not the End for character creation; the action point system and dice pool come from Cortex (Prime/Plus); the Powered by the Apocalypse games for narrative moves and clocks; Drakonym for character health management; and a few others. Thank you to all these excellent systems for their contributions. And above all, for weaving the threads that form the **fabric** of my creation.
