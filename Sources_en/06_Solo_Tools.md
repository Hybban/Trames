# VI. Tools for Solo Play

To make **THREADS** fully functional solo, this chapter offers a "narrative engine" that replaces the Game Master. These tools use the **d12** to maintain consistency with the rest of the system.

---

## 1. The Oracle of Fate
When you ask a closed question (where the answer is Yes or No) to the game universe, roll **1d12**:

| Result | Answer | Interpretation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 – 4** | **NO** | The event does not occur or the information is negative. |
| **5 – 8** | **YES, BUT...** | It happens, but with a nuance, a delay, or a complication. |
| **9 – 12** | **YES** | The answer is positive and the event unfolds in your favor. |

## 3. NPC Attitude
When you meet an NPC, determine their initial disposition with **1d12**:

* **1 - 2: Hostile.** Actively seeks to harm you.
* **3 - 5: Suspicious.** Uncooperative, asks for proof or guarantees.
* **6 - 7: Neutral.** Indifferent, deals with you out of necessity.
* **8 - 10: Friendly.** Open to discussion, ready to help.
* **11 - 12: Ally.** Very favorable, may offer a Resource or spontaneous help.

---

## 4. The Thread of the Story (Management Tips)

* **Logbook:** Note one sentence per scene to keep track.
* **Lexicon of Threads:** List the NPCs encountered and their current attitude.
* **Visualization:** Keep your clocks and wound boxes in plain sight.
