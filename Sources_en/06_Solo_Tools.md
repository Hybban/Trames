# VI. Solo Play Tools

For **TRAMES** to be fully functional in solo play, this chapter proposes a "narrative engine" that replaces the GM. These tools use the **d12** to maintain consistency with the rest of the system.

---

## 1. The Oracle of Destiny
When you ask a closed question (whose answer is Yes or No) to the game universe, roll **1d12**:

| Result | Answer | Interpretation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 – 4** | **NO** | The event does not occur or the information is negative. |
| **5 – 8** | **YES, BUT...** | It happens, but with a nuance, a delay, or a complication. |
| **9 – 12** | **YES** | The answer is positive and the event goes in your favor. |

## 3. NPC Attitude
When you meet an NPC, determine their initial disposition with **1d12**:

* **1 - 2: Hostile.** Actively seeks to harm you.
* **3 - 5: Suspicious.** Uncooperative, asks for proof or guarantees.
* **6 - 7: Neutral.** Indifferent, deals with you out of necessity.
* **8 - 10: Friendly.** Open to discussion, ready to help.
* **11 - 12: Ally.** Very favorable, can offer a Resource or spontaneous help.

---

## 4. The Thread of the Story (Management Tips)

* **Logbook:** Write down one sentence per scene to keep track.
* **Lexicon of Threads:** List the NPCs encountered and their current attitude.
* **Visualization:** Keep your clocks and your damage boxes in plain sight.

## 5. The Burnt Thread (The Ultimate Sacrifice)
The Burnt Thread is a tragic survival rule designed primarily for solo play, to prevent a premature death from breaking the narrative arc.

*   **Activation:** Usable only when facing a fatal outcome (final mortal wound or failure of an end-of-arc objective).
*   **Effect:** Your action is instantly transformed into a **Total Success (9-12)**. The danger is averted or the enemy defeated.
*   **The Price:** You must **permanently erase** one of your traits from your sheet. This trait is "burnt": it can no longer be used, rewritten, or increased.
    *   *Note on the Archetype:* If you burn your Archetype, your character survives but loses their essence. You must create a new Archetype with a score of **d6**, representing a broken hero who must rebuild themselves.

It should be possible to use it in group mode to avoid the tragic death of a character. In this case, the players will have to decide together which trait to sacrifice, and this sacrifice must have significant consequences for the rest of the story.
