# I. Introduction and Creation

TRAMES is a narrative and descriptive role-playing game system. It is designed to facilitate the rapid setup of campaigns and to allow for great flexibility in interpretation. In TRAMES, a hero's identity is defined by a set of traits that can evolve throughout the story.

**On the use of gender:** Around the gaming table, whether virtual or physical, there are people of all possible genders. But since they are people (personnes) and this word is feminine in French, we will have female players (joueuses) at the table. In the fiction, we will have characters (personnages) and this word is masculine. Therefore, regardless of their gender, the characters will be heroes. So simply put, when we speak of human persons, we use the feminine. When we speak of fictional characters, we use the masculine. Long live the French language. (Translator's Note: This paragraph explains the gender conventions used in the original French text. In English, we will generally use gender-neutral terms like "players" and "characters", or "they/them" pronouns where appropriate).

**Emotional safety:** Role-playing is above all a hobby, a pastime. The goal is to have a good time with friends. There is therefore no reason for anyone to feel uncomfortable or unsafe. This is why we recommend establishing safety rules for your table. These rules should be established at the beginning of each session, and recalled at the beginning of each campaign.

**Inspirations:** Not the End, Cortex (Prime/Plus), Powered by the Apocalypse, Drakonym, and a few others. Thank you to all these excellent systems for their contributions, even partial ones, to TRAMES. And especially for having woven the threads that weave the **fabric** (trame) of my creation.

## 1. Defining Traits
A hero is made up of a maximum of **eight traits** at the start. The character's number of traits may increase as their adventures progress. Although you can choose them freely, favor short and precise phrasing without being too restrictive. A trait can be an asset, but also a source of narrative complication (e.g., "Curious", "Devoted to their family").

Distribute your eight traits according to the following structure:

* **The Archetype (1 trait):** It defines your primary role or essence (e.g., "Battle Mage", "Repentant Thief").
* **Behavior (2 to 3 traits):** They describe your personality or your way of acting (e.g., "Natural Diplomat", "Suspicious of authority").
* **Abilities & Talents (the rest):** Your technical skills, powers, or special gifts (e.g., "Master Archer", "Keen senses").


## 2. Assigning Scores
Each trait has a die value that represents its maximum potential. At creation, you have a pool of dice to distribute among your eight traits:

* **1 trait at d12** (Reserved for the Archetype at character creation).
* **2 traits at d10**.
* **2 traits at d8**.
* **3 traits at d6**.

The value given to this trait is its current maximum value. This maximum value can evolve positively as adventures and tests progress. During play, the trait's value will fluctuate between its maximum value and d6.

> **Note on fluid creation:** You are not obliged to define all eight of your traits immediately. You can leave some "blank" and name them during play to meet a narrative need, drawing from the remaining dice in your initial pool.

## 3. Resources
In addition to their traits, a hero possesses between **1 and 5 resources** (unique items, contacts, social title, mount).

* **Narrative Use:** A resource can make an action automatically succeed if the situation allows (e.g., using a "Skeleton Key" for an ordinary lock).
* **Mechanical Use:** If you use a resource during a dice roll, you can **double one of the trait dice rolled** (roll the die twice and keep the better result before comparing it to the other traits).
* **Accessibility:** A resource can become inaccessible following a failure or complication.

> **Note on resources:** Resources are defined by the player, but must be validated by the Game Master to ensure they are consistent with the universe and the scenario. Also, just like for traits, it is possible to define resources during play according to narrative needs and respecting the limit of 5.

## 4. The Loom of the Fates: The Three Threads
At creation, you must define three narrative statements that represent the soul of your character. These threads interact with the token pool of the Loom.

* **Clotho's Thread (Conviction):** A sentence describing what your hero believes in or their moral compass. It's what pushes them to go beyond their limits.
* **Lachesis's Thread (Destiny):** A prophecy, a quest, or an ultimate goal. It's the direction their life is taking.
* **Atropos's Thread (Sin):** A vice, a weakness, or a dark instinct. It's what will cause their downfall or make them fall.

> **Note on Threads:** Threads are defined by the player, but must be validated by the Game Master, and the other players if necessary, to ensure they are consistent with the universe and the scenario. It is also possible to define Threads during play according to narrative needs, but beware, this should not be done lightly.
