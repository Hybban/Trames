# I. Introduction and Creation

THREADS is a roleplaying game where your hero is not defined by static statistics, but by descriptive traits that weave their identity. These traits serve as the foundation for all your actions and evolve throughout your story.

**On the use of gender:** At the gaming table, whether virtual or physical, there are people of all possible genders. To keep things simple and neutral in English, we use "the player" when referring to the real people playing the game. In the fiction, we use "the character" or "the hero" regardless of their gender.

**Emotional Safety:** Roleplaying is above all a hobby, a pastime. The goal is to have a good time with friends. There is absolutely no reason for anyone to feel uncomfortable or unsafe. That is why we recommend establishing safety rules for your table. These rules should be established at the beginning of each session, and be recalled at the start of every campaign.

**Inspirations:** Not the End, Cortex (Prime/Plus), Powered by the Apocalypse, Drakonym, and a few others. Thank you to all these excellent systems for their contributions, even partial, to THREADS. And above all for weaving the **threads** of my creation.

## 1. Defining Traits
A hero is composed of a maximum of **eight traits** at the start. The character's number of traits may increase as their adventures progress. While you can choose them freely, prefer short and precise formulations without being too restrictive. A trait can be an asset, but also a source of narrative complication (e.g., "Curious", "Devoted to their family").

Distribute your eight traits according to the following structure:

* **The Archetype (1 trait):** It defines your main role or your essence (e.g., "Battle Mage", "Repentant Thief").
* **Behavior (2 to 3 traits):** They describe your personality or your way of acting (e.g., "Natural Diplomat", "Distrustful of authority").
* **Abilities & Talents (the rest):** Your technical skills, powers, or special gifts (e.g., "Master Archer", "Keen Senses").


## 2. Assigning Scores
Each trait has a die value that represents its maximum potential. At creation, you have a pool of dice to distribute among your eight traits:

* **1 trait at d12** (Reserved for the Archetype at character creation).
* **2 traits at d10**.
* **2 traits at d8**.
* **3 traits at d6**.

The value given to this trait is its current maximum value. This maximum value can increase positively over the course of adventures and tests. During play, the trait's value will fluctuate between its maximum value and d6.

> **Note on fluid creation:** You are not obliged to define your eight traits immediately. You can leave some "empty" and name them during play to meet a narrative need, drawing from the remaining dice in your initial pool.

## 3. Resources
In addition to their traits, a hero possesses between **1 and 5 resources** (unique items, contacts, social title, mount).

* **Narrative Use:** A resource can make an action automatically succeed if the situation is appropriate (e.g., using a "Master Key" for an ordinary lock).
* **Mechanical Use:** If you use a resource during a die roll, you can **double one of the trait dice rolled** (roll the die twice and keep the best result before comparing it to the other traits).
* **Accessibility:** A resource can become inaccessible following a failure or a complication.

> **Note on resources:** Resources are defined by the player, but must be validated by the Game Master to ensure they are consistent with the setting and the scenario. Also, just like traits, it is possible to define resources during the game according to narrative needs and respecting the limit of 5.
