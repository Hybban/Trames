# TTRPG Design & Ergonomics Rules

When assisting with TTRPG writing and layout, rigorously apply these rules to ensure the output is professional, easy to navigate, and accessible during gameplay.

## 1. General Structure & Order
The manual should ideally follow this chronological flow so the reader learns progressively:
1. **Introduction to the Universe**: Brief "Fluff" to set the tone.
2. **Core Mechanics**: The resolution system (how to roll dice, base difficulties).
3. **Character Creation**: Only after the mechanics are understood can players make informed choices.
4. **Advanced Rules**: Magic, specific powers, detailed combat maneuvers.
5. **Game Master (GM) Section**: Tools, advice, bestiary, and campaign elements.

## 2. Writing Principles (Crunch vs. Fluff)
*   **Separation**: Clearly demarcate rules (Crunch) from lore (Fluff). Use formatting (headers, boxes) to visually slice them apart.
*   **K.I.S.S. (Keep It Simple, Stupid)**: Don't write 10 pages for a mechanic that takes 1 page to explain.
*   **The Lawyer's Fallacy**: Adding words does not add clarity. Use short sentences, bullet points, and bold **keywords**. Traquez l'implicite; explain everything logically.
*   **Examples are Mandatory**: Any complex or abstract rule MUST be accompanied by a brief, concrete in-game example.
*   **Forward References**: Avoid introducing terms or stats without explaining them or giving a clear page reference to where they are explained.

## 3. Ergonomics and Navigability
*   **Signposts & Tools**: Encourage the use of Table of Contents, Glossaries, and Indexes. Use clear headers and margin markers.
*   **Cheat Sheets**: The end of the book should include a quick-reference summary of core actions and an empty Character Sheet.
*   **Call-Out Boxes**: Use them religiously!
    *   **GM Secrets / Advice**: A box clearly marked for the GM.
    *   **Optional Rules**: A distinct box indicating rules that can be ignored for first-time play.
    *   **Rule Summary**: A quick bullet-point summary of a long mechanical paragraph.

## 4. Visual Layout (Maquette)
*   **Typography**: Stick to classical, highly readable fonts (Times, Helvetica) for the body text (min 12pt). Reserve "exotic" or gothic fonts strictly for headers.
*   **Whitespace**: Let the text breathe. Do not fill the page to the margins. A lack of margins makes it inaccessible for dyslexic and neuro-atypical readers.
*   **Contrast**: Avoid highly textured backdrops or illegible low-contrast text (like dark grey on black).
*   **Column Grids**: Default to a 2-column or 3-column grid for standard A4/Letter size to keep line lengths readable.
