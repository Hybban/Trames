## How to Roll

[Introduce the mechanical concept immediately here. Use short sentences.]

To attempt a risky action, roll **2d6 + your Attribute**. 
- On a **10+**, you succeed completely.
- On a **7-9**, you succeed, but at a cost.
- On a **6 or less**, the GM makes a hard move against you.

### Example of Play
> **GM:** The guard spots you picking the lock. What do you do?
> **Player:** I throw my smoke bomb and dive through the window! (Rolls 2d6 + Agility: 8).
> **GM:** You get through the window safely (Success), but you dropped your lockpicks on the sill in your haste (Cost).

## Modifiers and Difficulties

[Use bullet points or lists whenever possible to break down complex options.]
*   **Advantage**: Roll 3d6 and keep the highest two.
*   **Disadvantage**: Roll 3d6 and keep the lowest two.

<details>
<summary><b>[Encadré] GM Advice: Setting Difficulties</b></summary>
When setting a difficulty, don't penalize players for things their character is naturally good at. Instead of raising the difficulty, increase the severity of the consequences on a failure.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>[Encadré] Optional Rule: Gritty Realism</b></summary>
If your table prefers a more punishing game, any roll of 6 or less also inflicts 1 point of Stress.
</details>
